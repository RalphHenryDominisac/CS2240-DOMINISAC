Origin

   The idea of Microsoft creating its own gaming system gained traction in 1998, when Kevin Bachus, Seamus Blackley, Otto Berkes, and Ted Hase formed the company's first console team after successfully selling their concept to Bill Gates. The DirectX Box, as it was originally known, was supposed to be the first game system constructed like a PC, bringing all of the flexibility and capability of a gaming setup to the console industry.

The system would operate on Windows 2000, making it simple for traditional PC software developers to work inside the console's design, and would have more than twice the processing power of the current market leader, the PlayStation 2. Despite the marketing team's reservations, the abbreviated "Xbox" name was well received by focus groups, providing the firm with one of the final pieces it required to reveal its creation: a memorable name.

Consoles




XBOX
Console

    On November 15, 2001, Microsoft released the original large, black, and green Xbox in North America. It cost $300 and was released during the sixth console generation, making it the first significant game system developed by an American firm since the Atari Jaguar.

Controller

    The Duke was the name given to the controller that came with the system when it first came out. It established the standard for Xbox controllers to include two offset analog sticks and a D-pad, which was unusual at the time.

Specs

       CPU: Intel Pentium III Coppermine-based
       Memory: 64 MB DDR SDRAM at 200 MHz
       GPU: 233 MHz "NV2A" ASIC
       Weight: 4.65 kg



XBOX 360
Console

    On November 22, 2005, Microsoft introduced its second console in North America. At launch, the Xbox 360 was available in two versions. The higher-end Pro Edition cost $400 and came with a 20GB hard drive, but Microsoft also wanted to provide a $300 variant, so the Core model was produced.

Controller

    The Xbox 360 controller was a significant upgrade over the Duke controller. It used two offset analog sticks with a D-pad and was somewhat smaller than the Xbox's Controller S, but it removed two of the original controller's face buttons (moving from six to four) and replaced them with two bumpers.

Specs

       CPU: Xenon (a custom triple-core 64-bitP)
       Memory: 512 MB of GDDR3 RAM
       GPU: 500-MHz ATI Graphics Processor card
       Weight: 2.9kg




XBOX ONE
Console

    On November 22, 2013, Microsoft launched the eighth generation of consoles. The Xbox One was released at a price of $500, with the company touting it as the ultimate entertainment system and bundling a second-generation Kinect with every device. The updated Kinect has a 1080p camera with a broader field of view, better tracking accuracy, and heart-rate monitoring.

Controller

    The Xbox One controller's layout is similar to that of the Xbox 360's predecessor. Microsoft tweaked the ergonomics of the controller, added a gripping texture to the analog sticks, and increased the haptic feedback. Menu and View buttons took the role of the Start and Back buttons.


Specs

       CPU: AMD Jaguar 1.8GHz 8-core
       Memory: 8 GB DDR3
       GPU: AMD GCN Radeon 853MHz
       Weight: 3.5 kg






XBOX ONE X
Console

    The Xbox One X, a high-end hardware revision of the Xbox One, was first teased at E3 2016 under the codename "Project Scorpio" and released on November 7, 2017, with a 1 TB model priced at US$499 and a limited, preorder exclusive "Project Scorpio Edition" with a dark-gradient finish, vertical stand-brace, and green "Project Scorpio" inscriptions on the console and bundled controller.

Controller

    The controller went through three iterations, each with significant changes to the design and functionality. The Elite Wireless Controller, a premium version intended toward professional gamers with interchangeable parts and programmability capabilities, is also available from Microsoft. As a result, each of the aforementioned versions has been available in a variety of color schemes, some of which have unique designs tied to individual games.

Specs

       CPU: AMD Jaguar 2.3GHz 8-core
       Memory: 12 GB GDDR5
       GPU: AMD Radeon GCN 4.0
       Weight: 3.81 kg







XBOX SERIES X
Console

    The Xbox Series X is the fourth generation of the Xbox console family, succeeding the Xbox One. It was released on November 10, 2020. The Xbox Series X is a video game system from the ninth generation. The high-end model was first previewed at E3 2019 under the codename "Project Scarlett," before receiving its official name and design as Xbox Series X at The Game Awards later that month.

Controller

    It has a refined design with a slightly smaller body, a "Share" button below the "View" and "Menu" buttons in the center of the controller, a circular dished D-pad similar to the Elite Controller, and a USB-C connector instead of USB Micro-B. It also supports Bluetooth Low Energy and can be paired to both a Bluetooth device and an Xbox device at the same time.

Specs

       CPU: 8-core AMD Ryzen Zen 2
       Memory: 16GB GDDR6
       GPU: AMD Navi/RDNA 2-family GPU
       Weight: 4.45 kg
